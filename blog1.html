Determinant

Determinant is a number associated with each matrix. The number can tell a lot about the nature of the matrix.

Some uses of determinants are as follows

  • Test for invertibility: If the determinant of a matrix AAA﻿ is zero, then AAA﻿ is singular and not invertible.
  • The determinant of AAA﻿ equals the volume os a box in n-dimensional space.
  • The determinant gives a formula for each pivot. We can decide the step when a rwo exchange is required.

Properties of Determinant

  1. The determinant of identity matrix is 1
  1. The determinant changes sign when two rows or columns are exchanged.
  1. If two rows are equal then det(A)=0det(A) = 0det(A)=0﻿

Elementary Transformations

Elementary Transformations are key to solving any system of linear equations. While solving the problem in Gaussian Elimination, we have to make sure we do not encounter a 0 at a pivot which is critical. We can use the transformations to make sure we are consistent in the process. The transformations possible are:

  • Exchange of two equations (rows in the matrix).
  • Multiplication of an equation (row) with a constant λ∈ℜ−{0}\lambda \in \real - \{0\}λ∈ℜ−{0}﻿.
  • Addition of two equations (rows).
